The Channel Moat: Distribution + Know-How + Scale

  • Retrofit is PHYSICAL. Requires installation, calibration, hydraulic integration. Different CAN bus protocol on every brand of tractor.
  • Channel complexity is the real barrier. PTx has 70+ elite dealers, 200+ CNH dealer partners selling PTx products, and 100+ OEM partnerships. A new entrant (Bosch, Continental) would need to build this fragmented channel from scratch in a market too small and heterogeneous for automotive-scale players to justify. The know-how (18-month certification, multi-brand expertise) compounds the channel advantage.
  • Technology advancing faster = MORE retrofit demand. Farmer pays $15-40K for retrofit vs $350-500K for a new tractor.
  • OutRun works on Deere tractors. AGCO's autonomous grain cart directly monetizes Deere's installed base at $15K/yr subscription.
  • No per-acre recurring fees for core Precision Planting hardware. Farmers trust the brand because they own what they buy.

Precision Ag Penetration: Massive Whitespace Remains

Only 27% of US farms and ~25% of EU farms use ANY precision agriculture today. Computer vision and autonomy -- the next wave -- are below 5% adoption. The retrofit TAM is not theoretical; it is 73-75% of the developed-world farm base that has not yet adopted.

The Bull Case: From Selling the Tractor to Owning the Operating System

  • Tractor brands are sticky today because of physical lock-in -- parts inventory, dealer relationships, operator familiarity, multi-generational loyalty. 80% of farmers hope to transfer their operation to the next generation. The brand on the hood persists across decades.
  • Tomorrow, the precision ag platform will be stickier. The interactive system through which a farmer manages planting prescriptions, yield data, guidance settings, and autonomous operations becomes the true operating system. Once a farmer learns PTx's interface, calibrates their fields, and builds seasons of agronomic data, switching costs compound every year.
  • Fragmented ownership amplifies this. EU has 12.1 million farms averaging just 17.4 hectares -- too small to justify a $350-500K new tractor, but perfect for a $5-15K GPS retrofit, then a $10-20K planting upgrade, then $15-30K variable-rate application. Incremental adoption lowers the barrier: each upgrade is individually justifiable and builds on the prior investment.
  • AGCO retrofits competitors' tractors today → wins those customers' data and workflow loyalty → converts them to AGCO equipment purchases tomorrow. A farmer running PTx guidance on a John Deere tractor is already in AGCO's software ecosystem. When that Deere needs replacing in 5-10 years, the switching cost is now AWAY from the PTx platform, not away from Deere.
  • Farmers are fragmented, not corporatized. Unlike most industries, agriculture remains dominated by family operations protected by enormous political power -- CAP subsidies (EUR 307B), biofuel mandates, farm-state voting blocks that successfully blocked right-to-repair in 5 of 6 US states. This fragmentation means no single corporate buyer can standardize on Deere's closed ecosystem. Millions of independent operators will choose the open platform that works on whatever equipment they already own.
The analogy: Deere is building iOS -- beautiful, integrated, expensive, locked-in. PTx is building Android -- open, works on every device, wins on breadth and accessibility. In a fragmented market of 14 million farms (US + EU combined) where 73% have not yet adopted precision ag, Android wins.
Honest assessment: PTx leads in retrofit breadth (guidance, steering, planting hardware across 100+ brands) and cost (no per-acre subscription fees vs Deere's usage-based model). PTx lags in green-on-green spraying (SymphonyVision Duo is 4 years behind See & Spray) and data/AI (Deere has 330M+ engaged acres feeding models). The thesis does not require PTx to beat Deere on technology -- it requires PTx to be good enough for the 65% of the fleet that will never be Deere. Aftermarket competitors (Ag Leader, Topcon, Reichhardt) exist but lack PTx's breadth: no single competitor offers guidance + planting + spraying + autonomy in one mixed-fleet platform.
