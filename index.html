The platform play: AGCO retrofits a CNH or "other" tractor with PTx guidance and Precision Planting hardware. The farmer builds 3-5 years of yield data, planting prescriptions, and field maps on AGCO's platform. When that old tractor needs replacing, the switching cost is now away from the AGCO platform, not away from the old tractor brand. This is how you convert 65% of the global fleet from competitors to AGCO customers — one retrofit at a time.
The AI tailwind: I don't think the market has appropriately priced AI disruption in ag tech. In the next 12 months, examples of AI-driven precision ag (computer vision spraying, autonomous operations, yield prediction) will accelerate. This is bullish for AGCO because: (1) AI commoditizes the sensor layer — hardware becomes interchangeable, (2) platforms become easier to build and iterate on, (3) AGCO's open ecosystem benefits from AI faster than Deere's closed one, (4) but pure tech companies still can't enter because you need the heavy iron and the dealer network. SymphonyVision Duo already delivers 77% herbicide savings in beta — this catches up to Deere's See & Spray.

Why Sovereignty Is Bullish for AGCO

FactorImplication
Anti-trustEU regulators will resist further Deere market share gains. Deere's acquisitions (Wirtgen, HarvestLab) face scrutiny. A healthy European competitor (Fendt/AGCO) is in the EU's interest.
Domestic preference"Should the US be running European agriculture?" Fendt is manufactured in Marktoberdorf, Germany. Valtra in Suolahti, Finland. AGCO's European brands have European roots.
China protectionChinese manufacturers (YTO, Lovol, Foton) are expanding aggressively. The EU will protect premium European ag brands — just as it protects automotive (tariffs on Chinese EVs).
Data sovereigntyEU GDPR mindset extends to farm data. European farmers will increasingly demand that yield data, field maps, and prescriptions stay on European platforms.
CAP subsidies€307B in 2021-2027 cycle. Subsidies increasingly tied to precision ag adoption (eco-schemes). Fendt + PTx are best positioned to capture this.

The sovereignty thesis: Europe wants European companies running European agriculture. Fendt is German. Valtra is Finnish. AGCO's European manufacturing base is a structural moat that Deere cannot replicate through acquisition (anti-trust) or organic growth (brand perception). As Chinese competitors press into the value segment, the EU will protect its premium ag brands — and AGCO is the primary beneficiary.
